# PayWatch, explained simply (just for you)

Think of PayWatch as a smart friend who's good with money. You tell it what you earn, own, and owe — and it tells you how you're doing and what to do next, without the jargon. Here's the whole app in plain words.

---

## The one big idea: the Money Health Score

Everything centres on a single number out of 100 — like a "fitness score" for your money. A higher number means healthier finances.

It's made of **six smaller scores**, each worth a different amount:

- **Are you saving enough?** (the biggest one — counts for 25%)
- **Are you protected?** (insurance — 20%)
- **Are your investments spread out?** (not all in one basket — 20%)
- **Do you have a rainy-day fund?** (emergency money — 15%)
- **Is your debt under control?** (10%)
- **Are you smart about tax?** (10%)

The app scores each of these, then blends them into one number.

**One fair rule:** if something doesn't apply to you, it's left out. A student doesn't need life insurance, so the "insurance" part is simply ignored for them, and the other parts are weighted up to still add to 100. So the same score works for a 20-year-old student and a 50-year-old businessman.

---

## A few words you'll keep seeing

- **Take-home pay:** the salary that actually lands in your bank each month (after tax).
- **Surplus:** what's left after you pay your expenses. Money you *could* save or invest.
- **Deduction (tax):** money the government lets you subtract from your income before charging tax — so you're taxed on less.
- **SIP:** investing a fixed amount every month automatically (like a recurring deposit, but into investments).
- **Equity / debt:** "equity" = stocks and stock funds (grow more, but bounce up and down). "Debt" = safe stuff like FDs, PPF (steady, low growth).

## Now, page by page

### Overview (the home screen)
Your money at a glance. It shows your score, a "this month" summary (what to invest, your top task, next deadline), and a **growth picture**: where your wealth ends up if you keep doing what you do now, versus if you follow the plan. There's also a big button to **Ask PayWatch** a question.

### Ask PayWatch (the star feature)
A chat where you can ask anything about *your* money — "Should I prepay my loan or invest?" — and it answers in simple English using your actual numbers. It's an AI (Claude) that's been given your profile plus a library of finance facts. Two safety rules: it will **never** tell you to buy a specific stock or fund (that's illegal without a licence), and it won't promise "guaranteed returns." It sticks to general, honest guidance.

### Alerts
A little inbox where the app pokes you about things — a tax deadline coming up, a bill due, you spent more than usual, or "nice job, your score went up." So you don't have to remember everything.

### Actions
Your to-do list for money, sorted by what helps most. Each item is specific and has a rupee amount — like "build your emergency fund: add ₹1.3L" or "pay down your credit card." Tick one off and your score goes up. That little reward loop is the whole point.

### Net worth
Everything you own minus everything you owe = your net worth. The page shows what it's made of (a pie chart: how much is stocks, savings, property, gold), and a future projection of where it's heading.

### Invest
A simple plan for where to put spare money — **but only in general categories** (like "a large-cap index fund"), never a specific product. It first checks two things: do you have an emergency fund, and any credit-card debt? If yes, it tells you to fix those first (because paying off a 40%-interest card beats any investment). Then it suggests how to split your money based on your age and how much risk you're comfortable with.

### Markets & news
Educational reading — investment themes and finance news. No tips, no "buy this." Just to stay informed.

### Tax (an important one — explained simply below)
Shows how your tax works and how to legally pay less.

### File your ITR (the other important one — explained below)
A step-by-step wizard that does your whole tax return for you.

### Insurance
Checks if you're properly protected. Tells you, in plain terms, how much life and health cover you should have, what to buy, and what to avoid (it steers you away from those "investment + insurance" combo plans that are usually a bad deal).

### Statement scan
Upload your bank statement and it makes a neat spending report — where your money actually goes. Nice detail: the file is read **on your own phone/laptop**, never uploaded anywhere. (This is the real way to track spending right now; automatic bank-linking is coming later.)

### Document vault
A folder to keep tax/insurance papers, with reminders before anything expires.

### Goals
Set targets (a house, a car, retirement) and it tells you how much to save each month to get there.

### Reports
Generates documents you can save or hand to a CA — net-worth statement, tax pack, rent receipts.

### Plans
The paid versions. Free-ish "Starter" gets you the basics; "CFO" and "Family" unlock unlimited questions, the full tax engine, and more.

### Settings
Where you edit all your details — income, savings, loans, insurance, and your risk comfort level. This is the source of truth that feeds every calculation.

## Tax, in the simplest possible way

India has **two tax systems**, and you pick whichever charges you less:

- **Old system:** higher tax rates, BUT you can subtract lots of things first (investments, rent, insurance). Good if you have many deductions.
- **New system:** lower tax rates, but you can't subtract much. Good if you don't have many deductions.

PayWatch calculates your tax **both ways** and tells you which one wins and by how much. That's the headline of the Tax page.

**How tax is actually worked out** (same idea in both systems):
1. Start with your total income.
2. Subtract your deductions → this leaves your "taxable income."
3. Tax is charged in **slabs** — like buckets. The first chunk of income is taxed at 0%, the next chunk at 5%, the next at higher rates, and so on. You only pay the higher rate on the money *in that bucket*, not your whole income.
4. **The big relief:** if your taxable income is under about ₹12 lakh (new system), the government wipes your tax to **zero**. This is why most people earning up to ~₹12.75 lakh pay no income tax now.
5. Very high earners pay a small extra "surcharge," and everyone pays a 4% "cess" (a small top-up) on the tax.

The page also has a **Tax Copilot** — the year-round housekeeping a CA does: reminds you of deadlines, tracks which deductions you've used, and bundles everything into a neat pack.

**One simple example:** if you're in the 30% tax bracket and you invest ₹50,000 in something that's deductible, you save 30% of that = about ₹15,000 in tax. That's how the app shows you "this action saves you ₹X."

## "File your ITR," in the simplest way

Filing taxes sounds scary; this wizard makes it four easy steps:

1. **Income** — what you earned (salary, bank interest, any investment profits). You can even upload your Form 16 (the salary slip your employer gives) and it fills itself in.
2. **Deductions** — the things you can subtract (investments, insurance, home-loan interest).
3. **Tax already paid** — tax your employer/bank already cut from you during the year (called TDS).
4. **Result** — it shows the final maths: how much tax you owe in total, minus what you already paid, which tells you whether you get a **refund** (money back) or have to **pay a little more**.

It also:
- Picks the **right form** for you automatically (there are four types, depending on whether you have a business, capital gains, etc. — you don't need to know which; it decides).
- Gives you a **step-by-step walkthrough** to file it yourself on the government website in a few minutes.
- Or lets you **download a ready pack** to hand to a CA, who just checks and submits it.

**About investment profits (capital gains)** — because the wizard handles these too:
- If you sell stocks/funds you held **over a year**, the first ₹1.25 lakh of profit each year is **tax-free**; beyond that you pay 12.5%.
- If you sell within a year, the profit is taxed at 20%.

**Honest limit:** the app prepares and calculates everything, but for a few special cases (like a business that needs an "audit"), the law still requires a CA to sign. And submitting in one click from inside the app needs a special government registration we'll get later — for now it shows you exactly how to submit it yourself.

## How the "future wealth" numbers are figured out

When the app says "in 5 years you could have ₹X," here's the simple logic:
- It takes what you have now, assumes you keep adding money every month, and lets it grow at a yearly return.
- The **return depends on your risk level**: cautious ≈ 7% a year, balanced ≈ 9%, aggressive ≈ 11%. (These are on purpose a bit low, so it never over-promises.)
- "**If nothing changes**" = you keep investing what you invest today.
- "**With the plan**" = you invest about 25% of your take-home, increasing it a little each year.
- The difference between the two is the "opportunity" — what better habits could be worth.

It always reminds you these are *estimates*, not guarantees, because markets go up and down.

## The honest bits (good for you to know)

- It never tells you to buy a specific stock or fund — only general advice. That's a legal rule, and it also keeps the advice trustworthy.
- All the "future money" numbers are careful estimates, not promises.
- For tax, it does the heavy lifting but works *alongside* a CA where the law needs one — it doesn't pretend to replace them.
- Your bank statement is read on your own device and never uploaded.
